Emergency Services

Informational Demo Only. In real life-threatening situations, call local emergency services immediately.

What to do in an emergency

  1. Ensure your safety before attempting to help others.
  2. Call for help immediately (999, 998, 997) and state your location.
  3. Provide first aid within your scope, prioritizing breathing and bleeding.
  4. Do not move the injured person unless they are in immediate danger.

Build a Home Emergency Kit

  • Bandages & sterile gauze

  • Pain relievers

First Aid Quick Guide

  • Assess scene safety
  • Check breathing & responsiveness
  • Control heavy bleeding with pressure
  • Perform CPR if trained
  • Keep victim warm & calm

Common Emergencies & What to Do

Severe Bleeding
  • Apply firm pressure
  • Do NOT remove soaked bandages
  • Elevate if possible
  • Call emergency services
Burns
  • Cool with running water (10‚Äì20 min)
  • Do NOT apply oils or toothpaste
  • Cover loosely with a clean cloth
CPR (Adult)
  • Push hard & fast, center of chest
  • Rate: 100-120 compressions/min
  • Depth: 5-6 cm
  • Keep going until help arrives
Choking
  • Encourage coughing
  • Perform 5 back blows
  • Then 5 abdominal thrusts
